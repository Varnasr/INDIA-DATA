Key Empirical Findings:
  • Gender Asymmetry: 76.7% female surname dominance suggests significant participation gaps or systematic bias in registration patterns
  • Electoral Manipulation: Strong negative correlation (r = -0.74) between deletions and INDIA performance indicates potential systematic voter suppression
  • Religious Stratification: Muslim voters show 4.5:1 female-to-male ratio, significantly higher than Hindu populations (2.9:1)
  • Caste Concentration: Forward castes (Brahmin + Kshatriya) comprise 82.4% of identifiable surnames, indicating registration bias toward higher-status communities
  • Statistical Outliers: 1,313 booths with >250 deletions each represent 1.7% of total but account for 11.2% of all deletions

Caste-Based Electoral Patterns:
  • Forward Caste Dominance: 82.4% of identifiable surnames belong to Brahmin/Kshatriya categories, indicating significant upper-caste bias in voter registration
  • SC/ST Underrepresentation: Only 5.2% scheduled caste surnames despite 16% official population share in Bihar
  • Electoral Correlation: Forward caste dominated booths show 52.3% NDA preference vs 34.7% INDIA preference
  • Regional Stratification: Northern districts show higher forward caste concentration (>85%) compared to southern districts (68%)
  • Gender-Caste Intersection: SC/ST surnames show 89.2% female representation vs 76.1% for forward castes
